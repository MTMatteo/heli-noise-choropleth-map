Please, Don’t Get to the Chopper

Is helicopter noise, as tracked by 311 data, a city-wide problem?

By: Michael Matteo | December 19, 2022

On Thanksgiving day, 311 was inundated with calls complaining about helicopter noise. New York City’s helicopter industry, offering both tourist flights and air-taxi services to airports, is under scrutiny. City officials read off eye-popping figures at a City Council hearing after Thanksgiving. A 2,329% increase in helicopter noise complaints to 311 over the last five years, and an overall rise from 10,359 helicopter noise complaints in 2020 to 25,821 complaints in 2021.

At the hearing, Andrew Genn of the Economic Development Corporation suggested that more than half of helicopter noise complaints can be traced to tour companies based in New Jersey. Flights taking off from that side of the Hudson don’t have to follow NYC’s regulations for flying helicopters.

The above map, which tracked 311 data from last month, shows that most noise complaints came from the 10023 and 11414 ZIP codes. While complaints appear from other parts of the city, most of them are localized to neighborhoods near heliports.

Andrew Rosenthal, president of the non-profit Stop the Chop NY/NJ, said, “many [complaints] do come from the Upper West Side, 10023, 10024, and 10025 because they are subjected to all three sources of helicopters. The tourist choppers from NYC go up and down the river. The NJ tourists go directly over the UWS, often twice. And the commuter choppers also traverse the UWS.”
